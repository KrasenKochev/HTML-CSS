Healthy Spinach and Turkey Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna history

The origins of lasagna can be traced back to ancient Greece and Rome, where layered dishes of pasta and sauce were enjoyed.

Over the centuries, lasagna evolved into the iconic Italian comfort food we know today.

Our healthy spin on lasagna focuses on incorporating lean protein and nutrient-rich vegetables.

Lasagna health benefits

Our Healthy Spinach and Turkey Lasagna offers a range of nutritional benefits, making it a satisfying and guilt-free option.

  • Lean Protein: Lean ground turkey provides a protein-rich foundation for muscle support and energy.
  • Fiber Boost: Whole wheat noodles and fresh spinach contribute dietary fiber for digestive health.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: The combination of veggies offers vitamins A and C, as well as important minerals.
  • Heart-Friendly: Reduced-fat cheeses and lean protein promote heart health by minimizing saturated fats.
  • Low Calorie: With about 280 calories per serving, this lasagna fits well into a balanced diet.

Ingredients

  • 9 whole wheat lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb (450g) lean ground turkey
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 cup of low-fat cottage cheese
  • 1 cup of part-skim mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups of low-sodium marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil cooking spray

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil cooking spray over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add the minced garlic and lean ground turkey to the skillet. Cook until the turkey is browned and cooked through. Season with dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  3. Stir in the marinara sauce and fresh spinach leaves. Allow the mixture to simmer for a few minutes until the spinach is wilted.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the low-fat cottage cheese and shredded mozzarella cheese.
  5. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, start assembling the lasagna. Begin with a thin layer of the turkey and spinach mixture.
  6. Place three cooked lasagna noodles over the mixture.
  7. Spread a layer of the cottage cheese and mozzarella mixture.
  8. Repeat the layers, alternating between the turkey mixture, noodles, and cheese mixture, until all ingredients are used. Ensure the top layer is the cheese mixture.
  9. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the top.
  10. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for about 25 minutes.
  11. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbling.

Nutrition Information

Serving Size: 1 serving
Calories: ~280
Protein: ~25g
Carbohydrates: ~25g
Fat: ~9g
Fiber: ~4g
Sodium: ~400mg

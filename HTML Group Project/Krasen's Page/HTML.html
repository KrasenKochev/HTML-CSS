Sweet Potato Brownies With Chocolate Sauce

Our original recipe for sweet potato brownies was so popular that we've tried to make them even better for you.

We wanted something gooier, richer, more chocolaty and all round more indulgent and these are the result of that.

I promise you’d never know there was any veg in here at all!



Ingredients

For the brownies

  1. 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into small cubes
  2. 12 medjool dates, pitted
  3. 100g ground almonds
  4. 100g oat flour
  5. 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  6. 6 tablespoons cacao powder
  7. 6 tablespoons maple syrup
  8. 2 tablespoons almond butter, optional
  9. Water from 1x 400g can chickpeas
  10. 1 teaspoon baking powder
  11. Handful of dark chocolate chips
  12. Pinch of sea salt

For the chocolate sauce

  1. 1 teaspoon cacao powder
  2. 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  3. 2 tablespoons date syrup
  4. 4 tablespoons coconut milk, from a carton, or any other plant-based milk
  5. Pinch of sea salt

Method

  • Preheat the oven to 180c, fan setting.
  • Place the sweet potato into a steamer or in a saucepan with boiling water for 15-30 minutes, until they become really soft (the length of time will depend on the size of the chunks).
  • While the sweet potatoes cook, grind the oats in a food processor until they form a flour (if not already), place to one side.
  • Once the sweet potatoes are soft and beginning to fall apart, drain them and add them to a food processor with the pitted dates. Blend until smooth. Now add the remaining ingredients (except the chocolate) and blend until smooth. Once blended, stir through the chocolate chips (if using).
  • Spoon the mixture into a lined baking dish and cook for 45-50 minutes until you can pierce the brownie with a fork and bring it out clean.
  • Remove the tray and allow it to cool for at least 10 minutes – this is really important as it needs this time to stick together.
  • For the chocolate sauce, simply place all the ingredients into a pan over a medium heat. Stir continuously until everything is melted and has come together to form a smooth sauce, about 5 minutes. Pour on top of the brownies when ready to serve.
